Deep Learning and Artificial Intelligence

In the "Deep Learning and Artificial Intelligence" subject, I immersed myself in the fascinating world of neural networks and deep learning algorithms.
Through comprehensive coursework, I covered various topics, including the fundamentals of neural networks, backpropagation, optimization techniques, and advanced architectures like CNN, RNN, and LSTM. The subject delved into the mathematics behind deep learning and explored its practical applications in various business domains.






Assignment

Neural Networks Architecture (NNA)

I studied the architecture and working principles of neural networks, understanding how artificial neurons process and transmit information. I explored single-layer and multi-layer perceptrons, grasping their ability to solve complex problems through layering.


Mathematics of Deep Learning

I delved into the mathematical foundations of deep learning, including linear algebra, calculus, and optimization techniques, gaining insights into the optimization process and gradient descent.


Backpropagation and Loss Functions

I learned about backpropagation, a critical technique for training neural networks. Additionally, I explored various loss and cost functions that aid in evaluating the network's performance.


Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)

I studied the principles and implementation of artificial neural networks, discovering how they mimic the human brain's structure and can be used for pattern recognition, classification, and regression tasks.

Business Applications of Deep Learning

I explored real-world business applications of deep learning, including image recognition, natural language processing (NLP), recommendation systems, and anomaly detection. These applications showcased the versatility and power of deep learning in various industries.


Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN), LSTM

I delved into advanced deep learning architectures, understanding the unique capabilities of CNNs for image processing, RNNs for sequential data analysis, and LSTMs for handling long-term dependencies in sequential data.
