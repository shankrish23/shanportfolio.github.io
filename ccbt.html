Cloud Computing and Blockchain Technology

In the "Cloud Computing and Blockchain Technology" subject, I explored the dynamic landscape of cloud service providers and the potential applications of blockchain technology in enhancing business practices.
Through two assignments, one focusing on the "Cloud Wars Go Global" case study and the other on utilizing blockchain for product expiry date tracking, I gained valuable insights into cloud service competition and blockchain's practical implications.






Assignment

Cloud Wars Go Global" Case Study Presentation

In this assignment, I analyzed the competitive landscape among four major cloud service providers: Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Alibaba. I conducted in-depth research on their web services offerings, market share, and global expansion strategies. The presentation highlighted the strengths and weaknesses of each provider, allowing me to make informed recommendations for businesses seeking cloud solutions.


Utilizing Blockchain for Product Expiry Date Tracking - Pitch Deck

In this practical assignment, I designed a pitch deck showcasing how blockchain technology can be utilized to enhance supply chain management and ensure product safety by tracking expiry dates. The pitch deck outlined the benefits of blockchain's decentralized and immutable nature in maintaining transparent and accurate expiration date records for labeled products. It presented a viable solution to address the challenges of product expiration tracking and build consumer trust.
